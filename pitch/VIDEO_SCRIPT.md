# 🎬 ParkPulse — Demo Video: full shoot pack (judge-optimized script + delivery + wardrobe)

**Recommended length: ~3:00.** There's no hard cap this year, but keep it tight — judges watch many videos, and a sharp
3 minutes beats a loose 6. **Format:** ~20% talking-head (you on camera) + ~80% screen-recording of the live app with
voiceover. Judges often watch the video *first* and rank heavily on it — so the **first 10 seconds** and the **38%
moment** decide everything. Every beat below is built to land one of the four criteria: **Relevance · Feasibility ·
Innovation · Real-world impact.**

---

## 0. Before you record (read this twice)

**What to wear**
- A **solid, mid-tone shirt** — navy, deep teal, charcoal, or muted slate-blue (it echoes ParkPulse's blue accent).
  A **collar / smart-casual** reads as credible to government + corporate judges.
- **Avoid:** pure white (blooms on camera), black (loses your shape), bright red/green (clashes with the map's
  red/blue), busy stripes/checks/logos (moiré shimmer + distraction).
- Groomed, minimal accessories. As a team, **coordinate tones** (all smart-casual) — don't dress identically.

**Setup**
- **Light on your face, not behind you.** Face a window or a soft lamp; never a window at your back.
- **Background:** a plain wall or a tidy, slightly out-of-focus room. No clutter, no bright posters.
- **Camera at eye level**, framed chest-up. Phone is fine — prop it steady, don't hand-hold.
- **Screen recording:** 1080p, full-screen the app, hide bookmarks/tabs/notifications, browser at ~110% zoom so text is
  legible on a phone. Record the demo clicks *first and clean*, then lay voiceover on top.
- **Audio is half the score.** Quiet room, mic close (a ₹500 lapel mic or earbuds beat a laptop mic). No fan / traffic noise.

**How to deliver (expressions & energy)**
- **Look INTO the lens** on talking-head bits — that's eye contact with the judges. (Don't watch your own preview.)
- **Warm** on the problem, **crisp** on the tech, **proud** (not loud) on the proof. Smile genuinely at open and close.
- **Speak ~10% slower than feels natural.** Nerves speed you up — fight it.
- **Full one-beat pause after "38%."** Silence makes a number land.
- Natural hand gestures; don't fidget or cross arms. Sit/stand tall, chin level.
- **Rehearse 3–4 full timed run-throughs** out loud before the real take. Re-record any segment freely; you'll stitch them.

**⚠ Demo integrity rule (read this):** Only *click* things that **analyze the fixed 298,445 records** — the maps,
forecast, patrol plan, full-day planner, "why here?", "Also considered", WhatsApp/print. **Never click any
"add data / ingest / simulate challan" control on camera** — *describe* that capability in words. The dataset stays
fixed; showing data being added looks like tampering and risks disqualification. (The judge-facing UI has no add-data
button — it only describes the ingest pipeline — so you're safe, but don't go looking for one.)

**Time budget (3:00):** talking-head 0:00–0:12 and 2:50–3:00 (~22s); everything else is screen + voiceover.

## 1. Minute-by-minute script

> Format: **[TIME] — MODE — SCREEN action → SAY (verbatim).** Word counts are tuned to the time; don't rush.

### 0:00–0:12 · HOOK — *talking head, look at lens* — *(Relevance)*
**SAY:** "Every day, Bengaluru's traffic police fight illegal parking — and right now they fight it **blind.** A handful
of teams, a whole division, and a map in someone's head. We took **298,000** of their *own* parking tickets and turned
them into a system that says exactly **where to send those teams tomorrow.** This is **ParkPulse.**"

### 0:12–0:30 · THE PROBLEM — *screen: Command Center, slowly tilt the 3-D map* — *(Relevance)*
**SAY:** "Illegal parking near markets, metros and junctions blocks live lanes — the city's most visible congestion.
But enforcement is **reactive**: no heatmap, no priorities, no schedule. The data, though, has a pattern — the top
**one percent** of locations carry a **third** of every violation. The problem is *concentrated.* That's the opening."
*(let the tall red columns speak — point with the cursor)*

### 0:30–0:48 · THE HONEST INSIGHT — *screen: scroll to the coverage-by-hour bars* — *(Relevance + trust)*
**SAY:** "Now the part most teams would hide. This is **enforcement** data — where officers *wrote* tickets — not where
parking is worst. **Ninety-three percent** before 1 PM; almost nothing in the evening rush. So we don't fake a demand
model. We make the effort you already spend **sharper**, and we **flag the evening blind spot.** Honesty the people who
own the data can trust."

### 0:48–1:15 · SCORE + FORECAST — *screen: Forecast & Patrol; point at the r = 0.70 KPI* — *(Innovation + feasibility)*
**SAY:** "ParkPulse is a closed loop — **detect, score, forecast, deploy, target.** Every zone gets one **transparent**
score: how many violations, how severe, how flow-critical. Then we forecast load for every zone, weekday and hour — and
we validate it the **hard** way: trained on the past, tested on weeks it never saw. Correlation **point-seven-zero** —
real signal, not an overfit. We even beat a gradient-boosting model, and kept the one we can explain in a sentence."

### 1:15–1:42 · DEPLOY + TRANSPARENCY — *screen: Sat · 9–13 · 8 teams → plan → tap a team ("why?") → glance at "Also considered" → click WhatsApp* — *(Real-world impact + innovation)*
**SAY:** "Here's what an officer actually uses. Pick a shift and the number of teams — ParkPulse places each one on the
highest-impact, **well-spaced** corner. Tap a team: it tells you **why it's there.** Look just below: it shows the strong
zones that **just missed**, and exactly why. No black box — it's auditable both ways. Then send the whole plan to a
**WhatsApp** patrol group, or export a CSV for roll-call. Instant. Deployable. Today."

### 1:42–2:08 · THE PROOF ⭐ — *screen: Coverage & ROI → the "Proven impact" card; hold on the 38% bars* — *(Real-world impact)*
**SAY:** "Does it actually work? We replayed a **ten-team plan** across **thirty-one days the model had never seen.**
Those ten teams would have been standing on **thirty-eight percent** of the violations that actually happened —"
*(PAUSE — one full beat)* "— versus **one-point-three percent** spread evenly. That's not a claim. That's **tested**, on
data it never saw."

### 2:08–2:32 · THE CO-PILOT — *screen: Ask page; click the mic and SPEAK the query* — *(Innovation)*
**SAY (into the app's mic):** "Plan six teams for Friday evening near KR Market." *(plan + map render)*
**SAY (voiceover):** "And no officer has to learn a dashboard. They just **ask** — by voice, in **English, Hindi or
Kannada.** That runs the *real* forecaster and optimizer and answers with a real plan and map. It's an **agent** over
our models, not a chatbot — and it remembers the conversation."

### 2:32–2:50 · SELF-IMPROVING + INTEGRITY — *screen: Full-day Planner (/day) — morning 361 → evening 2 + the reallocation nudge* — *(Innovation + feasibility)*
**SAY:** "It's built to get **better.** The full-day view finds the evening blind spot **from the data itself** — and
tells you to shift a couple of low-value morning teams into it. Deploy there, those catches become new data, and the gap
starts to close. New challans flow through a live ingest that rebuilds the models in seconds — the architecture for a
nightly feed. And to be clear: we **never touch** the fixed competition data — that pipeline is for live BTP data."
*(DESCRIBE the ingest — do NOT click any add-data control. 2-min cut: "The full-day view finds the evening blind spot
and tells you to shift teams into it.")*

### 2:50–3:00 · CLOSE — *talking head, look at lens, calm smile* — *(Feasibility + impact)*
**SAY:** "It runs **today** — a Next.js web app backed by a FastAPI service, on data BTP **already** collects, no new
hardware. **ParkPulse** — the live pulse of the city's parking, turning what already happened into **where to stand
tomorrow.** Thank you." *(hold the smile 2s; end on the title card + the live app + repo links)*
